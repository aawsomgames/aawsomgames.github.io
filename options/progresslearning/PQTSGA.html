Practive Questions: The Second Great Awakening

Changes in which of these social ills were called for by the Second Great Awakening?

Temperance of alcohol, public education for the non-wealthy, and women's efforts to gain suffrage

How did the Baptist faith change during the early to mid 1800s in the American South?

It became more accepting of the use of slaves.

Which of these was a lasting impact from the Second Great Awakening of the 19th Century in America?

People began to apply Christian principles to the social ills of society.

This movement, which swept America in the early 1800s, was the first time that people began to consider how to apply religious beliefs to social problems.

The Second Great Awakening

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints (The Mormons) fled persecution in the Midwestern U.S. in the 19th century, eventually settling in

Utah.

Which group was known for its belief that a direct experience of God was available to all people and that organized church services were unnecessary?

The Quakers

Which of these social changes in the U.S. in the 1800s could be attributed to the ideas presented in this passage?

The abolitionist movement

Which period would be MOST associated with the Second Great Awakening in United States history?

1800s-1820s

The Abolition Movement largely coincided with what major American religious movement?

The Second Great Awakening

This passage most closely aligns with which of these movements?

The Second Great Awakening
